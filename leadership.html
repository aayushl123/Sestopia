Leadership

  • Skill Classification: Non-technical/Soft-skill
  • Related Software Engineering Area(s) : Software Engineering Management

"Leaders perceive future outcomes to proactively react to present situations while in contrast managers aim for stability and try to solve the problem by executing existing ways." [1]

Leadership is known as “the process of influencing the activities of an organized group in its efforts towards goal setting and goal achievement.” [2]

Leadership is a skill that a person must exhibit to be successful in every aspect of his/her life. Leadership skills are necessary whenever there is a group of people involved, small or big, for example, running a family, running a company or running a country. Any group without a good leader is doomed to collapse and fail. There is a popular misconception that “Leaders are born, not made”. But on the contrary, most of the great leaders of this world became great through hard work and experience. The ability to effectively lead, motivate and direct a group of people – whether it is in business, sport or politics - requires a very complex set of skills, mostly acquired through experiences, self development as well as access to subsequent training.


When it comes to Software Engineering, it is often associated with hard-skills such as programming, debugging, testing, and so on. While that is true to some extent, it cannot be ignored that soft skills are also vital to software engineering as a discipline. Leadership in software engineering can be in many forms - teamlead, project manager, product manager, etc. This document explores the various aspects of how leadership skills are important in software engineering.

Prerequisites

Leadership skills in the software industry can come in many forms. Some of which are discussed below.

  • Technical skills

    • A leader must be able to identify and understand the technological challenges in a project and select team members with the specific skills for the project.

  • Decision-making skills

    • A leader should be able to make decisions in critical situations. These decisions may be complex decisions like which programming language to choose or emotional ones like the termination of a low performing employee. In any case, a leader must be bold enough to make these decisions.

  • Interpersonal skills

    • Interpersonal skills are necessary for a leader to effectively communicate his/her ideas to the team or senior management and to build a trusting and friendly relation towards the team.

  • Product development skills

    • A leader should have sufficient knowledge on software product development such as software life cycles, software engineering processes, etc. so that he/she can take informed steps towards the effective delivery of a project.

Besides these five must have skills for a leader, there are few other skills that a good leader needs to posses. [3] [4]

  • Visionary
  • Accountability
  • Delegation
  • Empathy
  • Motivating
  • Inspiring
  • Empowering
  • Recognizing success
  • Problem solving
  • Communication
  • Building a team

Rationale

Software Engineering knowledge and good technical skills

First and foremost, to be a good leader in any domain, you need to have sufficient technical knowledge about that particular domain. With a graduate degree in software engineering and three years of industrial experience, I believe I fulfill this criteria to be an effective leader.

Logical and analytical thinking

Logical and analytical thinking are inherent to a software engineer. As a software engineer in a leadership role, I believe I can apply the same rigorous logical analytic thinking towards the technical and management tasks and improve my skills further.

Ability to make quick decisions and set priorities

The ability to make decisions and set priorities follows logical and analytical thinking. In order to make critical decisions, one must be able to analyze all possible options and choose a rational one. I believe this is one of my strongest suits which is why I think I can make a good leader.

People person and good interpersonal skills

Needless to say, a good leader must have good relations with the people working with or under him/her. A leader must be able to listen well, communicate proactively, be polite and personable, and act as a friend and a mentor. As a leader, I will have the chance to communicate with more people and can sharpen my interpersonal skills further.

Roles

Leadership skills in software engineering can be applicable on different levels, from leading a small team to leading a global enterprise. Some of these roles are discussed below. [5]

Technical Lead

A technical lead is a software engineer who is usually more experienced than his/her peers and guides the technical vision and execution of a software project. Their area of influence includes technical direction, implementation choices, coding styles, the establishment of best practices, and technical standards.

Team Lead

A team leader is a technical lead for all projects that a particular team takes on.

Engineering Manager

An engineering manager is a software engineer who has chosen a management career track over a technical one. He or she has up to ten direct reports, but preferably no more than six or seven. He or she spends a significant part of their time coding and the remaining on people management and leadership activities.

Director of Engineering

The director level is traditionally the first of a series of executive roles. It is common for a director of engineering to provide technical guidance, write code and participate in low-level discussions about the architecture and implementation of systems.

Chief Technological Officer (CTO)

In general, the responsibilities of a CTO depends wildly on the size of the company. It can include product vision and being the public face of the company’s technology. It can also include being responsible for identifying, leverage and integrate new technologies and drive business strategy and revenue.

Chief Information Officer (CIO)

The CIO is an operation-oriented executive who is internally facing and focused on improving the bottom line by setting strategy and direction to enhance the company’s internal infrastructure and technical operations.

Activities and Artifacts

Set a vision

Leadership vision is an essential means for focusing attention on what matters most; what you want to accomplish in your life and what kind of leader you wish to be [6]. A leader in the software engineering industry must be able to set vision for his team, department, company branch or an entire franchise.
For example, an e-commerce software company might the vision "To make shopping fun and easy." This kind company-wide vision is set by the leaders in the executive role such as a CTO or CIO.

Setting a vision for a team or a company might require a vision document.

Motivate employees

A leader must figure out ways on how to motivate employees or team-members to accomplish work goals. There are numerous ways in which a leader can motivate his/her employees or peers such as recognizing success, providing positive feedbacks, be respectful and polite. Besides these, material motivations such as rewards, vacations, incentives, promotions can be even more effective to motivate employees.

Identify needs

A leader must be able to identify the needs of his/her employees, peers or team members. In some situations, employees might not be able to or do not want to openly state what they need or want. In such cases, a leader must be capable enough to recognize such situations and resolve it accordingly.

Performance reviews

Conducting performance reviews is necessary for the growth of a company or a team. It is a must that a well-performing employee be appreciated with incentives such as promotion or salary increase and low-performing employees be penalized which might even lead to termination. This is a hard reality in every industry and a leader must be bold enough to make such harsh decisions when needed.

This process might include producing performance report for a team or even for the entire company.

Real-World Example/Scenario

The following section provides a brief description on how the leadership skills and vision of certain individuals created and shaped the biggest technological giants of the modern age.

Bill Gates

When it comes to leadership in the 21st century, there is no better example than Bill Gates. Under his leadership, the biggest tech-giant of the modern day - Microsoft was born. His vision and ideas redefined personal computing by making it cheaper and easily accessible to general public. This video further explains how Bill Gates' leadership skills led to him becoming one of the richest men on the planet.

Elon Musk

According to 2019 Global Brand Health Report published by Hired, Elon Musk was voted as the most inspiring leaders in technology. Elon Musk is the CEO of many tech companies among which SpaceX and Tesla are the most famous ones. His vision for SpaceX is to build and settle human colonies on Mars, which proves that not only is he a tech leader but also a leader for the entire human race.

Larry Page

Larry Page is an internet entrepreneur and computer scientist, who co-invented the PageRank algorithm which was used to develop the most widely used search engine of the 21st century - Google. Under his vision and leadership, Google created other famous products like Gmail, Google Maps, YouTube, Android OS, etc. which are one of the most used products over the internet.

Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was one of the most successful entrepreneurial leaders of the last half-century. He was highly successful, both as an entrepreneurial founder and after leading Apple to corporate greatness after the company had become stagnant for more than a decade under different leadership [8]. Under Steve Jobs' vision, Apple created some highly successful products like Macintosh, Mac OS X, iTunes, iPod, iPhone, etc.

Role of Academia/Industry

Academia: The computer science or software engineering curriculum in most of universities are primarily focused on the hard/technical skills and soft skills are mostly ignored. The fact that most of the academic projects do not have a designated leader proves that leadership skills are not given much attention in academia.

However, every academic team project I have come across usually has an unsaid leader. He/she is not voted as a leader by the team or picked by the teacher as a team leader, but will be doing most of the works that a leader does. This can include calling project meetings, asking and reporting on progress, getting in touch with the teacher to clarify requirements, and so on. Showing such traits in multiple academic projects one after another can really help develop leadership skills in a student.

This example alone shows that, although, the academic curriculum does not give much attention to developing leadership skills, one can attain it by acting as a leader in academic projects.

Industry: In the above section about four tech leaders, three of them were high-school/college dropouts. Using these examples, it can fairly be said that these great tech leaders culminated their leadership skills through hardships in the industry rather than during their time in academic institutions.

There are two ways to attain leadership in industry. The first one being starting as a developer and rising above the ranks as shown above in Roles section - technical lead, team lead, engineering manager and so on. This is a long process and does not always guarantee the top position. However, you can still develop valuable leadership skills staying at the lower levels and leading the team at that level. One does not always need to be at a top management position to be a good leader.

The next way is to do what the tech leaders mentioned in the above section did - become an entrepreneur and start your own company. This way you can really develop your leadership skills.
