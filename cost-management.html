Cost Management

  • Skill Classification: Technical
  • Related Software Engineering Area(s) : Software Engineering Economics

Project cost management is an essential part in software engineering process. Effective project cost management allows each project to be specific and unique because that project entails costs and requires specific funding. It begins at the planning phase of the project. When the planning of specific project begins, a cost manager will estimate the actual cost of finishing the software project based on the software structure and visual design. If the project have a retentively high risk of running beyond budget, the cost manager will negotiate with the team to change the original design of the project.

In practise, the process includes cost estimation, budget determination and spending control.

Cost Estimation. It is the project cost management process step when the project manager cooperates with the financial department to estimate costs required for purchasing all necessary good/services and undertaking necessary activities to deliver the project. Project Cost Estimation is conducted at the planning phase. The project manager uses project cost management software to develop spreadsheets and make calculations.

Budget Determination. At this step of the cost management process, cost spreadsheets are used to develop the budget framework and determine the budget. The project manager can use project cost management software to work in collaboration with the financial department to determine items of the budget and sources of funding and then to allocate the budget. The step entails close cooperation with the project sponsor.

Spending Control. It is the step of the project cost management process when the allocated budget is reviewed and spending is tracked. The project manager takes responsibility for control spending and to ensure that the budget allocation is optimized and costs are fully covered with the planned and allocated budget.

Prerequisites

  • Precise understanding of the design and structure of the project
  • Clear view of the projects intend clients and expecting profits
  • Vast programming experience through different languages and environments
  • Negotiating skill between design and development team
  • Overall understanding of server hardware performance

Rationale

Without Cost Management, what will happen?

As it should be, all fantastic ideas come with a price, either in time or in resources, without cost management, a project team may continuously make changes to their software using new and attractive tools and styles, which may never earn profits from the intended users. Or a project team may wrongly estimate the time cost of their product, and may miss their opportunities, and lose in the market.

Cost Management skills can build up through the progression of time

The cost managing skill can’t be improved by only reading papers or taking lessons, since there is no identical projects, and it’s hard to make precise estimation of cost beforehand, so it’s a skill highly related to experience. I think experience is the only critical things can’t be learned through documents.

Roles

Software Developer

Software developer is the one who knows most about the development environment and should be aware of the budget and the possibilities of running out of budget.

Software Designer

A good software designer should not only think about the actual look and functionality of certain product, but also consider about its cost to make the program running.

Product manager

A product manager should have a clear of the cost of certain product, thus apply resources to get things done.

Prototyping and PR team

A prototype, when showed before public, the PR team should be aware of the cost of that prototype and make sure the prototype does not make user think too high of the final product, showing a prototype of a good yet couldn’t be finished prototype is not always a good decision.

Activities and Artifacts

Cost management process should follow a discipline, and it mainly take place by 4 steps:

1. Resource Planning. At the planning stage of the project, we should make cost management plan based on the ideas of the team or the need of clients, resource planning then takes the estimated resource quantities, evaluates resource availability and limitations considering project circumstances, and then optimizes how the available resources (which are often limited) will be used in the activities over time. The optimization is performed in an iterative manner using the duration estimating and resource allocation steps of the schedule planning and development process.

2. Cost Estimating. Based on historical data, try to make cost estimation of the project. It involves the application of techniques that convert quantified technical and programmatic information about an asset or project into finance and resource information. The outputs of estimating are used primarily as inputs for business planning and decisions for schedule control processes.

3. Cost budgeting. At the implementation stage of the project, we should try to make use of our resources and also keep track of all the resources we are using, thus lower the possibilities of running over budget.

4. Cost control. After each milestones have been reached, review the process and document the cost of project by time and resources for future use. Based on the progress and the cost, make necessary changes to the software implementation plan. All changes to the cost baseline need to be recorded and the expected final total costs are continuously forecasted. When actual cost information becomes available an important part of cost control is to explain what is causing the variance from the cost baseline. Based on this analysis corrective action might be required to avoid cost overruns.

Real-World Example/Scenario

F-22 Raptor Fighter Aircraft

The numerous new technologies in the F-22 resulted in substantial cost overruns and delays. Many capabilities were deferred to post-service upgrades, which reduced the initial cost but increased total program cost.

Montreal Olympics

The Montreal Olympics is not successful because of its high budget overrun. Estimation saying that the Montreal 1976 Summer Olympics has a cost overrun at 720% in real terms, which lead to a big loss 2.4 of billion dollars.

Role of Academia/Industry

Cost management is essential in both academia and industry. It helps us manage our budget and keep the project in progress. One should continuously record the use of all the resources to prevent budget overrun in academic research. In industry, managing cost is also managing revenue, doing cost management can make the company focus more on the key features of the product, which may help lower the cost of production, thus gain more profits.
